## Key Signals

- Median full resolution time is still 742.5 hours in March, after a major January spike to 3,173.1 hours. Ask what is sitting open so long — backlog, complex cases, or process bottlenecks.
- First reply time moved from 2.8 to 5.1 hours across the window. Worth asking what changed operationally — staffing, routing, or channel mix.
- Email is 52% of volume and API is 23%. Ask whether they want to shift more demand to messaging, automation, or self-service.
- Self-service ratio is 0.00 across all six months. Directly probe whether the help center is inactive, hard to find, or not trusted by customers.
- CSAT is not enabled. Surface that they currently have no direct customer sentiment signal on support quality.

---

## What's Already Working

Performance has been stable over the past 6 months. Ticket volume is steady at ~805 avg closed tickets per month, and zero-touch improved from 10.1% to 22.7% across the window. The opportunity is forward-looking — especially around self-service, automation, and faster resolution.

## ROI Analysis

**Assumptions:** 17 agents · €33.0/ticket · AHT 203 min · €37.5k loaded salary · 8h/day · 48-week year · 6-month avg volume used · Deflection 5/15/25% · Productivity 5/15/25% · Growth: 6.1% · 1 USD = 0.8559 EUR · Public salary estimate based on Spain sources, loaded multiplier 1.5x from public employer cost ranges

### Pillar 1 — Ticket Deflection

A 15% reduction could deflect ~121 tickets/month → ~€47.8k/yr (~$55.8k/yr)

| Scenario | Tickets/Month | Monthly Savings | Annual Savings | Annual (USD) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative (5%) | ~40 | ~€1.3k | ~€15.9k | ~$18.6k |
| Moderate (15%) | ~121 | ~€4.0k | ~€47.8k | ~$55.8k |
| Strong (25%) | ~201 | ~€6.6k | ~€79.6k | ~$93.0k |

### Pillar 4 — If Ticket Growth Continues, What Happens?

At the current pace, the business may face ~49 extra tickets per month.

| Choice | What It Means |
|---|---|
| Do nothing | The current team absorbs the pressure, risking slower service and backlog growth |
| Hire | Would require ~2 additional agents → ~€75.0k/yr extra cost |
| Push existing team harder | Productivity would need to rise from 47 to ~50 tickets/agent/month → ~6% increase |
| Automate the growth | Automation absorbs the extra workload instead of new hires → ~2 FTE avoided → ~€19.4k/yr avoided future cost |

> At the current growth rate, doing nothing creates pressure. Hiring is expensive. Productivity alone is unlikely to close the gap. Automation is the clearest scale path.
